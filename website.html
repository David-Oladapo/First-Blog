Become a Product Designer:
Grow Your Career Successfully

Want to kickstart your career as a Product Designer? We've written a practical
guide on what you need to do to become a product designer and land a job in 2023.

  • Design
  • Product
  • 8 min read

Let's get one thing out of the way: you don't need a fancy Bachelor's Degree to get into Product Design. In fact, more companies hiring Product Designers, UX Designers, Graphic Designers, and Web Designers in 2022 are completely disregarding tertiary education requirements. These companies are now hiring employees based on their skills and experience, rather than whether or not they graduated from college.

"If you have the skills and a good portfolio, you'll
be able to land job interviews."

- Olivia Rhye, Product Designer

While learning to become a Product Designer is not an easy career path, there's never been a better time to get into the design industry. Large companies such as Stripe, Malichimp, and intercom are increasingly recognizing good design as a strong differentiator, so skilled designers and there's never been more affordable online courses and resources to learn.

Because of surging demand, it's also an increasingly lucrative career to get into; Product Designers and UX Designers in the United States earn an average base salary of $94,530. At Himalayas, we've seen Senior Product Design jobs listed with salaries of $240,000 and above.

In this article, we'll cover the practical steps you can take to begin your career as a Product Designer, with tips, resources, and advice on how succeed and land your first job. To start with, we should clearly understand what a Product Designer is and what they do.
